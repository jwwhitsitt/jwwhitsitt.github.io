// The Ethel Problem

Every great business has an Ethel.

She knows every regular by name. She remembers that Mr. Henderson likes a callback before noon. She has been the face of your business longer than your logo has. Customers ask for her specifically.

Ethel is irreplaceable.

But Ethel goes home at five. Ethel is only human -- and even the best humans occasionally let a lead slip through the cracks while juggling three things at once.

That is not a flaw. That is biology.

Our agents work the hours Ethel does not. They catch what slips through the cracks. They handle the repetition so Ethel can focus on the moments that actually need her.

Don't fire Ethel.
Give her a little backup.

And if you do not have an Ethel yet? The agent holds down the front desk until you are ready to hire her.
